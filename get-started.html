What You Will Need

A crochet hook. Generally speaking size 6 is the best all round hook and we find bamboo is the best material to work with, though metal or plastic will work fine.

Yarn. Start of with a material such as acrylic. It is cheaper and you can easily reverse mistakes - wool is not as forgiving!

Time. Crocheting is a skill that takes practice and patience. Give yourself enough time to learn and donâ€™t be put off if things are not working for you right away. They will with practice and time.

Your own space. Find somewhere comfortable and quiet where you can relax and focus fully on learning your new skill.

Start of by holding the hook between your thumb and index finger. Hold the hook firmly to stop it slipping out of your grip.

Hold the yarn in your opposite hand, pinching the yarn between your thumb and index finger. You will keep your index finger free to help manoeuvre the yarn.

Make sure to keep your yarn hand parallel to your hook hand so that you can keep a consistent tension in your stitches.

One of the most basic stitches in crocheting is the Half Double Crochet (HDC) stitch. Start of by yarning over, and insert the hook into the next stitch. This will give you 2 loops on the handle of the hook.

Yarn over again, and pull up a loop using the hook. There should now be 3 loops on the handle of the hook.

Yarn over one more time, and pull the yarn through all three loops on the handle of the hook. All 3 steps can be viewed in the video below.
Congratulations, you have now completed your first stitch - the HDC. Well done!
